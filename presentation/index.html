Welcome

Welcome to the Harris-Nelson Family Reunion

This reunion started when Mr. John Harris and Ms. Judy Bender united a long time ago. Both were born in the late 1800s — he was a farmer, and she was a homemaker.

Their union brought forth six children, four boys and two girls: Gussy, Pasty, Josh, A.C., D.B., and Will Harris.

"As I call your name, please stand with your family!"

Why we gather

  • To honor the people who got us here

Our Family History · Part 1

From One Union, Many Branches

1

John Harris & Judy Bender (born late 1800s) — six children: Gussy, Pasty, Josh, A.C., D.B., and Will Harris.

2

Emma (Bently) Moncrief (1867–1954) & Ed Moncrief had 16 children. Out of these 16, Mandy Moncrief (c. 1900–1986) was born. Mandy married Nelson Price (b. 1894) and blessed us with Lela Mae Price (July 16, 1922 – February 15, 1996).

3

Lela Mae Price married Dennis Bernard "D.B." Harris — three girls: Mary Nelson, Mildred Lucile Harris (1942–2012), Jessie Mae Harris-Moore. She later married Floyd Nelson and had six more children: James Edward, James Earl (mom liked the name James!), Floyd Nelson Jr, Mary Jane Harris (Gray), Mandy Louise Ellis, and the baby of the bunch, Shirley Harris.

4

Charlie Nelson & Mandy Harris brought forth two boys: Floyd Nelson (the oldest) and Joe Nelson. Joe married Oreatha Ward — four children: Doris Joe Smith, Mandy Mary Thomas, Charles Nelson, and the baby, Naiomi Matthews.

Family Business · Governance

We're Adopting Robert's Rules of Order 📜

Robert's Rules keep meetings fair, short, and drama-free: one person speaks at a time, every voice gets heard, and decisions get made by vote — not by volume.

  • Quorum — we set a minimum number of members to conduct business
  • Motions — "I move that we…" puts an idea on the floor
  • Second — one other member says "second" to open discussion
  • Discussion — the chair recognizes speakers, pro and con, in turn
  • Vote — majority carries; the Secretary records the result

Cheat sheet: what do I say?

You want to…You say…
Propose something"I move that…"
Support it"Second!"
Change the wording"I move to amend…"
End the debate"I move the previous question"
Take a break"I move to recess"
Wrap it up"I move to adjourn"

A one-page Robert's Rules quick guide will be posted on the website and handed out at every business meeting.

Family Business · Decade Planning

Where We Meet Next 🗺️ — Halfway Between Jackson, MS & Cleveland, OH

The drive is about 930 miles, so the true halfway point lands around Nashville, TN / Bowling Green, KY. These lodges host reunions with rooms, cabins, banquet space, and catering all on one property:

🎯 At the halfway mark

  • Montgomery Bell State Park — Burns, TN (45 min from Nashville): 117 lakefront lodge rooms, cabins, 7,000+ sq ft of event space, pavilions up to 200
  • Barren River Lake State Resort Park — near Bowling Green, KY: lodge + 22 two-bedroom cabins, banquet room seating 224–400, beach & golf

🚗 Along the route (KY / IN / SW OH)

  • Kentucky Dam Village State Resort Park — Gilbertsville, KY: lodge, cottages, marina; markets itself for family reunions
  • General Butler State Resort Park — off I-71 between Louisville & Cincinnati
  • Brown County State Park / Abe Martin Lodge — IN: 80+ rooms, cabins, indoor water park
  • Hueston Woods Lodge — College Corner, OH

🏙️ Closer to Cleveland

  • Salt Fork State Park Lodge — Lore City, OH: lodge, cabins, beach, catered banquet rooms
  • Punderson Manor Lodge — Newbury, OH: Tudor manor + 26 two-bedroom cabins
  • The Lodge at Geneva-on-the-Lake — Lake Erie shore, 8,500 sq ft of event space

How we decide

The Planning Committee gets group-rate quotes from the top three picks (headcount, beds vs. banquet needs, time of year), and we choose by member vote — a motion, a second, and a show of hands. Decade planning means we can rotate: halfway one reunion, closer to each hub the next.

Family Business · Our Land

Getting Our Family Land Back 🏡

We are partnering with Where Is My Land (whereismyland.com) — an organization that helps Black families research, document, and reclaim land that was taken from them, through genealogical research, records work, advocacy, and legal pathways.

  • Step 1: Gather what we know — deeds, tax records, oral history, locations
  • Step 2: Engage Where Is My Land and fund the retainer for the research/claim work
  • Step 3: Family volunteers support the research (a volunteer questionnaire is ready)

How reunion fundraising will be split

  • 🏡 A share of every fundraiser goes to the land-back fund — the Where Is My Land retainer plus renovation & revitalization of our land
  • 🌱 A share becomes seed money for future reunions
  • 🎓 A share grows the scholarship fund (next slide)

Exact percentages to be set by member vote today — chair, entertain a motion!

If our claim succeeds, recovered land/proceeds would be stewarded by the family foundation (501(c)(3) slides ahead).

Family Business · New Program

Helping Hands — the Family Hardship Fund 🤝

Family takes care of family. Each reunion, we award aid to a member walking through a hard season — chosen by confidential application, not by who asks loudest.

Who can apply

  • Any descendant, spouse/partner, or child (minors via a guardian) — or apply on behalf of a relative
  • Hardship within the past 12 months: medical, job loss, housing/disaster, funeral, caregiving, education emergency
  • One award per household per cycle; dues status does not affect eligibility

How the recipient is chosen

  • Confidential application → Philanthropy & Fundraising Committee scores it
  • Rubric: severity & urgency 40% · impact of the award 30% · clarity & completeness 20% · committee discretion 10%
  • 3+ independent scorers; household conflicts recuse; officers ratify
  • Recipient may stay anonymous — always their choice

Deadlines each cycle

  • Applications open January 1 of the reunion year
  • Deadline: 30 days before the reunion
  • Committee scores by 14 days out; officers ratify by 7
  • Award presented at Sunday brunch 🏆

Applications: hnfamilyreunion.com (Hardship Fund page) or paper from any officer — handed directly to the committee. Award amount set by the Treasurer's budget each cycle: $[vote today]. Funded alongside the scholarship fund from our fundraising split.

Family Business · The Foundation

Starting Our Family Foundation — a 501(c)(3) 🏛️

In the next few months we will form a nonprofit foundation so donations are tax-deductible and our scholarship, land, and legacy work has a permanent legal home.

The process (next few months)

  1. Month 1: Choose the name, elect the founding board, draft bylaws & conflict-of-interest policy
  2. Month 1–2: File Articles of Incorporation with the state; get an EIN (free, IRS.gov)
  3. Month 2–3: File IRS Form 1023-EZ (if eligible — expected under $50k/yr) or full Form 1023
  4. Month 3+: State charity registration, open a bank account, adopt an annual budget

Foundation board (to be elected)

  • Board Chair · Vice Chair · Board Secretary · Board Treasurer
  • 3–5 at-large directors — one from each generation if we can
  • Officers of the reunion may serve, but the foundation board is its own body with its own votes
  • Board meets quarterly; minutes published to the family

Nominations open today — see the Secretary. We'll ratify the founding board by motion and vote under Robert's Rules.
