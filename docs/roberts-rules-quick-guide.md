# Robert's Rules of Order — One-Page Family Quick Guide

*Hand this out at every Harris-Nelson business meeting.*

## Why we use it
One person speaks at a time, every voice gets heard, and decisions are made by
vote — not by volume. Meetings stay short, fair, and drama-free.

## The flow of every decision
1. **Motion** — a member says: *"I move that we…"* (puts an idea on the floor)
2. **Second** — another member says: *"Second!"* (shows at least two people want to discuss it)
3. **Discussion** — the President recognizes speakers in turn, alternating pro and con
4. **Vote** — usually voice vote ("all in favor say aye"); majority carries
5. **Record** — the Secretary writes the motion and result into the minutes

## Key terms
| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Quorum** | Minimum members present to conduct business (we set this in our bylaws) |
| **The floor** | Your turn to speak — get it by being recognized by the chair |
| **Amend** | Change the wording of a motion before it's voted on |
| **Table** | Set a motion aside for later |
| **Point of order** | "We're breaking our own rules" — the chair must rule on it immediately |
| **Adjourn** | End the meeting |

## What do I say?
| You want to… | You say… | Needs a second? | Vote needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Propose an idea | "I move that…" | Yes | Majority |
| Change the wording | "I move to amend the motion by…" | Yes | Majority |
| End the debate and vote now | "I move the previous question" | Yes | 2/3 |
| Set it aside for later | "I move to table the motion" | Yes | Majority |
| Take a break | "I move to recess for 10 minutes" | Yes | Majority |
| Flag a rules problem | "Point of order!" | No | Chair rules |
| End the meeting | "I move to adjourn" | Yes | Majority |

## House rules for the Harris-Nelson family
- The **President (Miesha)** chairs; the **Vice President (Aunt Shirley)** chairs in her absence.
- The **Sergeant-at-Arms (Joseph)** keeps order — one conversation at a time.
- Speak **twice max** per motion, **2 minutes** per turn, until everyone who wants a first turn has had one.
- Committee chairs give a **3-minute report** each meeting; questions after, not during.
- Money motions must state an **amount and which fund** it comes from, and require the
  Treasurer's balance confirmation before the vote.
