Give to the Family's Future 💛

Every dollar raised at the reunion is split three ways by family vote: our land, our students, and the operating fund that keeps this family running — including this very website.

The Three Funds

🏡 Land-Back Retainer Fund

We're partnering with Where Is My Land — an organization that helps Black families research, document, and reclaim family land — to pursue the return of our family's land. This fund pays the retainer and research costs — and the renovation and revitalization of family land: repairs, restoration, cleanup, surveys, taxes, and improvements.

🎓 Scholarship Fund

Scholarships for descendants of our ancestors — college, trade school, and certifications. Applications open to the whole family; awards presented at the reunion banquet.

Why the Land Fund Matters

The story

Like far too many Black families, land that belonged to our people slipped out of the family's hands. Where Is My Land specializes in exactly this: tracing the records, documenting the taking, and pursuing every available path — legal, legislative, and negotiated — to get land back or win restitution.

Our plan

  • Gather deeds, tax records, and oral history from the elders (bring documents to the Family Tree table!)
  • Fund the retainer through reunion fundraising
  • Family volunteers support the research — fill out the volunteer questionnaire on the Committees page
  • Any recovered land or proceeds will be stewarded by our family foundation (501(c)(3) in formation)
