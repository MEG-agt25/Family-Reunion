Where We Come From

"As I call your name, please stand with your family!" — brought to you by Yours Truly, That Aunt 💛

The Founding Union

This reunion started when Mr. John Harris and Ms. Judy Bender united a long time ago. Both were born in the late 1800s — he was a farmer, and she was a homemaker. Their union brought forth six children, four boys and two girls: Gussy, Pasty, Josh, A.C., D.B., and Will Harris.

The Line of Lela Mae

The Moncriefs & the Prices

Emma (Bently) Moncrief (1867–1954) and Ed Moncrief had sixteen children. Out of these sixteen, Mandy Moncrief (c. 1900–1986) was born. Mandy married Nelson Price (b. 1894) and blessed us with Lela Mae Price — born July 16, 1922; she carried this family until February 15, 1996.

Lela Mae's Nine

Lela Mae Price (1922–1996) married Dennis Bernard "D.B." Harris, and three girls were born: Mary Nelson, Mildred Lucile Harris (1942–2012), Jessie Mae Harris-Moore. She later married Floyd Nelson and had six more children: James Edward, James Earl (mom liked the name James!), Floyd Nelson Jr, Mary Jane Harris (Gray), Mandy Louise Ellis, and the baby of the bunch, Shirley Harris.

The Nelsons

Charlie Nelson and Mandy Harris brought forth two boys: Floyd Nelson, the oldest, and Joe Nelson. Joe married Oreatha Ward — four children: Doris Joe Smith, Mandy Mary Thomas, Charles Nelson, and the baby, Naiomi Matthews.

And the branches keep growing…

Mary married Willie Nelson in 1957, Mildred married William Thomas Ellis, Jessie married Simon Derrick in 1965 — and from there, dozens of children, grandchildren, and great-grandchildren. That's what the Family Tree Project is for: getting every single name recorded.
