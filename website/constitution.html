Constitution & Bylaws 📜

Our parliamentary authority is Robert's Rules of Order (Newly Revised). Items marked [like this] are choices the family votes on.

Constitution

Article I — Name

The name of this organization shall be the Harris-Nelson Family Reunion Organization (the "Organization").

Article II — Purpose

  1. Preserve, document, and celebrate the history and heritage of the descendants of John Harris & Judy Bender, the Moncrief–Price line of Lela Mae Price, and the Nelson family lines;
  2. Plan and hold a family reunion on a regular schedule;
  3. Support the education of descendants through a scholarship fund;
  4. Pursue the research and recovery of family land, including partnership with Where Is My Land;
  5. Establish and support a charitable family foundation (501(c)(3)); and
  6. Strengthen the bonds of family across every branch and generation.

Article III — Membership

  1. Members. All descendants of the ancestors named in Article II, their spouses/partners, and their children are members.
  2. Voting members. Members aged [18] or older who are current on dues (or granted a hardship waiver by the officers) may vote.
  3. No discrimination. Every branch and generation has an equal seat at the table.

Article IV — Officers

The elected officers are: President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer, Financial Secretary, Historian, Hospitality Chair, Sergeant-at-Arms, and Membership Chair. Their duties are defined in the Bylaws.

Article V — Meetings

  1. The Annual Business Meeting is held during the family reunion (or by video call in a year with no reunion).
  2. Robert's Rules of Order (Newly Revised) is the parliamentary authority for all business meetings, in all matters not covered by this Constitution and the Bylaws.

Article VI — Amendments

This Constitution may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of voting members present at an Annual Business Meeting, provided the proposed amendment was distributed to the family at least [30] days in advance.

Bylaws

Article 1 — Duties of Officers

  1. President — presides at all meetings; sets the agenda with the Secretary; appoints committee chairs (subject to confirmation by member vote); represents the Organization.
  2. Vice President — presides in the President's absence; supervises and supports all committee chairs; receives monthly committee reports.
  3. Secretary — records and keeps minutes; maintains the roll of members; sends meeting notices at least [14] days ahead; keeps this Constitution & Bylaws current with adopted amendments.
  4. Treasurer — has custody of all funds; pays approved expenses; signs with a second authorized officer on amounts over $[500]; presents a written financial report at every business meeting.
  5. Financial Secretary — receives and records all money coming in (dues, shirt payments, donations); issues receipts; reconciles records with the Treasurer monthly.
  6. Historian — maintains the family history, archives, yearbook, photo collections, and the family tree (including the Ancestry tree).
  7. Hospitality Chair — leads welcome and comfort at all events; elder care, ushers, and assigned tables; counts superlatives ballots.
  8. Sergeant-at-Arms — maintains order at meetings and events under the direction of the chair; supports the Safety Committee.
  9. Membership Chair — maintains the membership roll with the Secretary; recruits and welcomes new members; matches volunteer sign-ups to committees so that every committee has 2–5 members assisting its chair.

Article 2 — Elections & Terms

  1. Officers are elected at the Annual Business Meeting by majority vote and serve a term of [two years / until the next reunion], with a maximum of [two] consecutive terms in the same office (waivable by 2/3 vote).
  2. Nominations are taken from the floor; a member must consent to serve.
  3. A vacancy is filled for the remainder of the term by majority vote of the remaining officers, subject to ratification at the next business meeting.
  4. An officer may be removed for cause by a 2/3 vote of voting members after written notice and a chance to respond.

Article 3 — Meetings & Quorum

  1. Annual Business Meeting: held during the reunion (Sunday session).
  2. Officer meetings: at least quarterly, by call or video.
  3. Quorum for the Annual Business Meeting is [25] voting members or [20%] of voting members, whichever is fewer; for officer meetings, a majority of officers.
  4. Between meetings, routine decisions may be made by officer majority; any expense over $[500] not already budgeted requires a member vote or unanimous officer approval reported at the next meeting.
  5. All meetings are conducted under Robert's Rules of Order (Newly Revised); a one-page family quick guide is provided at every meeting.

Article 4 — Dues & Finances

  1. Reunion dues are set by member vote on recommendation of the Treasurer and Planning Committee (2026: $125 single or with 1 minor; $175 single + guest or child in college; $225 family of 3+).
  2. All funds are deposited in the Organization's bank account; payments are accepted by Zelle to the family account, Cash App, or through a family representative, with payer and purpose noted.
  3. The fiscal year runs [January 1 – December 31].
  4. Fundraising split. Unless changed by member vote, net fundraising proceeds are divided: [___%] land-back fund, [___%] scholarship fund, [___%] seed money for the next reunion.
  5. The land-back fund covers the Where Is My Land retainer and related research and legal costs, and also the renovation and revitalization of family land — repairs, restoration, cleanup, surveys, taxes, and improvements that keep the land productive and in the family.
  6. The books may be reviewed on request by any two voting members not related by household to the Treasurer or Financial Secretary.

Article 5 — Committees

  1. Standing committees: Sports/Games; Music; Food; Safety; Services; Photography; Philanthropy & Fundraising; Planning; Technology.
  2. Chairs are appointed by the President and confirmed by member vote; each committee has 2–5 members drawn from volunteer sign-ups.
  3. Committees meet monthly; chairs report to the Vice President and give a report at every business meeting.
  4. Committee spending requires a written budget request approved by the Treasurer before funds are committed.
  5. Special committees and sub-committees (e.g., decade planning, venue scouting, scholarship review) may be created by the President or by member vote, and dissolve when their work is done.

Article 6 — The Family Foundation

  1. The Organization will establish a separate charitable foundation under Section 501(c)(3) per the roadmap adopted by the family.
  2. The Foundation has its own board and bylaws; Organization officers may serve on the Foundation board, but the two bodies vote separately and keep separate funds and books.
  3. Charitable funds (scholarship fund, land-back fund) transfer to the Foundation once it is legally able to receive them.

Article 7 — The Reunion

  1. The reunion is held [every year / every two years], rotating locations by decade plan adopted by member vote.
  2. The host city's members form the core of the Planning Committee for that reunion.
  3. Reunion dates, dues, and budget for the next reunion are voted at the Annual Business Meeting.

Article 8 — Amendments to the Bylaws

These Bylaws may be amended by a majority vote of voting members present at an Annual Business Meeting, provided notice of the proposed change was sent to the family at least [14] days in advance.

Article 9 — Dissolution

If the Organization dissolves, remaining funds shall be transferred to the Foundation if it exists; otherwise, they shall be divided per a final member vote toward scholarships and family-history preservation. No funds shall benefit any private individual.
