How We Run Meetings — Robert's Rules of Order

The basics

  • Quorum — a minimum number of members must be present to conduct business
  • Motion — "I move that we…" puts an idea on the floor
  • Second — one member seconds to open discussion
  • Discussion — the chair recognizes speakers in turn, pro and con
  • Vote — majority carries; the Secretary records the result in the minutes

What do I say?

You want to…You say…
Propose something"I move that…"
Support it"Second!"
Change the wording"I move to amend…"
End the debate"I move the previous question"
Wrap it up"I move to adjourn"

The Family Foundation — Our 501(c)(3)

The roadmap (next few months)

  1. Month 1: choose the name, elect the founding board, draft bylaws & conflict-of-interest policy
  2. Month 1–2: file Articles of Incorporation with the state; obtain an EIN (free at IRS.gov)
  3. Month 2–3: file IRS Form 1023-EZ (if eligible) or Form 1023 for tax-exempt status
  4. Month 3+: state charity registration, bank account, first annual budget

The founding board (to be elected)

  • Board Chair · Vice Chair · Board Secretary · Board Treasurer
  • 3–5 at-large directors — ideally one per generation
  • The foundation board is its own body with its own votes, separate from the reunion officers
  • Quarterly meetings; minutes published to the family

Interested in serving? Sign up and check "Philanthropy & Fundraising," or email the Secretary.

Once approved, donations to the foundation (scholarships, land fund) become tax-deductible.
