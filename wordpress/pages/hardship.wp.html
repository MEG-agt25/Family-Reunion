Helping Hands — the Family Hardship Fund 🤝

Family takes care of family. Each reunion, the family awards aid to a member (or members) walking through a hard season — reviewed confidentially, decided fairly, given with love.

How It Works

1 · Apply (confidential)

Any family member facing hardship — or someone applying on behalf of a family member — submits the application below or the paper version. Only the review committee sees it.

2 · Committee review

The Philanthropy & Fundraising Committee scores every application against the published rubric. Committee members recuse themselves from applications from their own household. Officers ratify the selection.

3 · The award

Presented at the Sunday brunch each reunion. Recipients may choose to be recognized or to remain completely anonymous — their choice, no exceptions.

Eligibility Criteria

  • A member of the family — a descendant of our ancestors, or their spouse/partner or child (minors apply through a parent/guardian)
  • Experienced a significant hardship within the past 12 months: medical, job loss, housing or disaster, funeral expenses, caregiving, or an education emergency
  • A complete application — supporting documentation is welcome but not required
  • One award per household per reunion cycle; past recipients are eligible again after [2] cycles
  • Dues status does not affect eligibility — hardship is exactly when dues are hardest to pay

How the Recipient Is Chosen

RubricWeight
Severity & urgency of the hardship40%
Impact the award would make right now30%
Clarity & completeness of the application20%
Committee discretion10%

Every application is scored independently by at least three committee members; scores are averaged. Ties go to a committee vote. All materials are destroyed or returned after the cycle.
